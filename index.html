Introduction

What I am & where I want to be

As a person, I value honesty, optimism and faithfulness in myself and others. During my first year of Bachelor of Commerce course, I realised that I enjoy statistical analysis, which led me to a career in data science. My vision is formed around what I enjoy. I want to secure a full-time role as a data scientist, where I can use data to find insights that help drive decision making. To work towards this vision, I chose Business analytics and Econometrics as my majors to gain most of the hard skills required for a data scientist.

The following is an analysis of Tomlinson's (2017) graduate capital model, highlighting aspects that I am very strong at and those that still have a lot of potential for improvement.

Human Capital
I have acquired the majority of the technical skills necessary to work as a professional data scientist throughout my time studying at Monash University. I can foreground the links between the skills that I have acquired and the requirements of the jobs in the market. I do need to work on developing a few skills, though. Python programming is the most crucial ability I need to master because, according to Loon (2022), it is the programming language most frequently demanded for a data science position. I have a solid background in R but very little experience with Python because R was the primary programming language used when I was in school.

Social Capital
I have already established strong connections with my family and friends from high school before joining Monash. I have grown my network by forming connections with certain people who share similar interests during my time at Monash. But because of the pandemic, it was challenging to develop relationships, and as a result, my network did not grow as much as it should have. I think I still need to concentrate on expanding my network, especially with people in my field. Not so long ago, I created a LinkedIn profile to help me grow my network and keep tabs on any emerging career opportunities.
Moreover, working at Amazon warehouse for over a year has helped me develop good teamwork and communication skills, which will be beneficial for my career. I've developed close bonds with both my manager and my co-workers.

Cultural Capital
I have made a diverse number of friends and colleagues and have shown my broad understanding by being part of several diverse teams. My appreciation and comprehension of many cultures have grown as a result of this exposure. My understanding of cultures had grown while I was working at Amazon, which made it simple for me to fit in at work. I should keep an open mind going forward and be willing to expand my comprehension and adaptability in order to continue to develop.

Identity Capital
I have made some developments in my identity capital. By being introspective, I have identified my strengths and areas that need improvement. Over many years, I wondered why I feared of being judged, lacked confidence in giving my opinions, and feared of trying. Just recently, I learned that all this is because I have low self-esteem. Going forward, I need to boost my self-esteem and try to reform any other weaknesses I have in order to develop my 'employable self'.

Psychological Capital
The pandemic has allowed me to be more resilient while coping with change in relation to moving to online studies. I have been proactive in advancing my career through online education rather than waiting to continue my studies till everything went back to normal, as some people have done. I learned to tolerate uncertainty and withstand setback. Moreover, working full-time at Amazon and studying full-time while maintaining my grades at High Distinction, I demonstrated that I could perform well under pressure.

What I've learned

Creativity
When it comes to creativity, many people think about art and imagination. However, creativity has a different meaning in a commerce degree. Shch (2020) defines creativity as “a way of thinking that inspires, challenges, and helps people to find innovative solutions and create opportunities out of problems” (para. 2). Both innovation and inspiration come from creativity (Shch, 2020).

In BEX3500, completing the major project required creative skills. Creative skills were required to help understand how construction companies, like Laing O’Rourke, have a negative impact on life below water. These skills were also required to come up with innovative ways on how the company can reduce its impact on marine life. Moreover, I have demonstrated creativity in BEX3500 by producing websites for the major project and this assessment.

Some of the important attributes of creativity are:
Curiosity - Always asking “Why?” helps one to get a better understanding in a particular concept, which could lead to innovation of new ideas. To grow as a data scientist, curiosity is important because it would help me to better understand the context of the project I would be working on. Asking questions will help in brainstorming and could also help in building relationships with my colleagues, improving my social capital.

Risk-taking - Being creative involves the willingness to take risks in relation to trying out new ideas. You do not know if an idea is bad until you test it. Creative people tend to learn from their mistakes and improve upon them in the future. Data scientists experiment with ideas like using new models or creating new features and sometimes these ideas may not help in improving the model outcome. Therefore, taking risks is vital for data scientists. Being a risk-taker will strengthen my psychological capital as I would learn to withstand setbacks.

Critical thinking
Critical thinking involves gathering information and analysing it in various ways (evaluate from many angles, consider advantages and disadvantages) to make informed decisions (Oxford College, 2021). BEX3500 has required the use of critical thinking skills several times. Firstly, developing a logical recommendation for the major project required evaluating the pros and cons of several solutions we had and deciding on which solution to recommend. In addition, critical thinking was required to reflect on my strengths and weaknesses regarding Tomlinson's (2017) Graduate Capital Model. Moreover, conducting the informational interview allowed me to critically analyse whether I really want to pursue a career in data science.

Some important attributes of critical thinking are:
Open-mindedness - Keeping an open mind allows critical thinkers to hear others' points of view and opinions. I conducted the informational interview by keeping an open mind. Listening to the interviewee's view on data science cleared my doubt on this career. In the future, listening to other people's views could also clear some doubts that I could have. As a data scientist I will have to work as a team and will have to listen to others' opinions on the project at task regarding what model to be used or which variables to use. This will help build relationship, developing my social capital.

Introspective - Being introspective enables one to better understand oneself by critically analysing one's own strengths and weaknesses. The informational interview allowed me to be introspective and think about what I need to do to become a professional data scientist. I realised which skills I still need to develop. Being introspective in the future will allow me to understand my weaknesses and find ways of improvement, thus developing my identity capital.

Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)
The SDGs provide some kind of framework that businesses across the world could use for decision-making. As a commerce graduate, learning about the SDGs is important to develop an understanding of the significant global issues, getting inspired to take positive actions.

Before BEX3500, I had never heard of the SDGs. This unit provided me an opportunity to learn about the SDGs and why they are important. I believe I can help advance the sustainability goals and outcomes. As a data scientist I could provide measures on how the business is performing in relation to the SDGs and provide statistics that could help in making decisions regarding the SDGs.

What I need to do to get there

In order to achieve my vision, I would need to develop my graduate capital as outlined by Tomlinson (2017), especially human, social and identity capitals.

Human Capital
In terms of my human capital, I plan to complete an honours year in 2023 to develop further knowledge that would be advantageous for my career. My goal is to acquire a first-class honours degree in business analytics in 2023.

Moreover, I need to improve my programming skills in python because it is listed as a required skill in most jobs of data science. My goal is to develop python skills by June 2023. There are several python courses available online. To achieve this goal, I will partake a few of these courses and then participate in some Kaggle competitions to apply my new skills to some data science problems.

In addition, I would also need to develop knowledge in some additional data science tools. According to Data Flair (2022), some of the data science tools widely used in 2022 are SAS, Apache Spark, BigML and MATLAB. It is very difficult to learn all these tools in a short time, so I plan to learn these tools slowly as I progress my career.

Data science is an evolving discipline. There is always new concepts or tools coming in. The growing nature of data science could be a potential setback because it is almost impossible to master data science. The best I can do is keep track of the trends in data science and keep learning as I progress my career.

Social Capital
Regarding my social capital, I need to expand my network to include people within the same field. I plan to partake in a graduate program at Deloitte in 2024, ideally in risk advisory. Through this program, I will be able to connect with people who share my interests, growing my network and advancing my career. Moreover, it will provide me an opportunity to get to know what attributes and behaviours are valued by the employers, improving my cultural capital.

A potential setback could be getting rejected for the program due to failing to meet the requirements. To remedy this, I would need to double check whether I have met all the requirements of the program. I could also apply to other graduate programs as a backup.

Identity Capital
As I continue to progress into my career, I should perform self-reflection to identify and evaluate my strengths and weaknesses both professionally and personally. I would need to develop strategies to strengthen my weaknesses to help develop my work identity. I would also need to evaluate any strengths and weaknesses in any of the other capitals and take action to improve on them as I progress into my career. One of the ways to help identify my strengths and weaknesses is getting feedback from others because other people might reveal some information that I am not aware of. Moreover, other people could also give some advice on how to strengthen the weaknesses.
